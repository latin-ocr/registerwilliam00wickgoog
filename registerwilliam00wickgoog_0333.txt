to the archbishop. After thanking him for conferring a
pension of ten marks a year on his clerk, Robert de
Lavannia, “racione nove creacionis vestre,” until he should
be otherwise provided for by the archbishop, he now requires
him to confer the same on Master Walter de Brettone,5
“quia karissime amice nostre, domine regine Francia, et

merita, specialiter habemus recommendatum, et cujus
promocionem pleno corde affectamus.”
